My Rationale

Hi. I'm Paul Moss. I'm a product management and web development consultant. This site is my tool to find more work when my clients don't have anything to work on. It can be your tool too.

I created this tool because there were tons of freelance/job sites on the internet and none that did what I needed to help cut through the noise. Most of them are full of full time positions with a few freelance or consultant roles mixed in. Even if there are roles for contract work, much of that is not in one of the technologies I specialize in. I sort through all of that to find just the listings that are for contract work in my area and then I go through that (much, much smaller) list manually.

Freelance Finder aggregates information from tons of sites on the internet and then deletes nearly all of it. Full time jobs: deleted. Junk posts: deleted. Unreasonable budgets: deleted. All of the junk is deleted and only the quality listings remain. Then a simple list of the things that seem like possibilities are presented in an easy to delete way. Critically, there is an end to the job search process every day when there are no more jobs to look through. Then I can get to work or just relax and move on to other things.

Even with a network of clients, you still need to do a lot of legwork to keep the pipeline of new jobs filled. If you're busy with clients or a digital nomad busy traveling, you don't have to spend time searching for your next lead. Instead of sifting through stale or low quality postings to find your next client, save time job hunting every day and simply use Freelance Finder.

I aggregate from as many job sites as I can find, but also from twitter, google results, emails I get, everything. My goal is to find the best freelance jobs available no matter where they are listed.

I invite you to come use my tool to sift through the noise and find the freelance/contract work that is right for you. It will save you time and energy that could be better spent on paying gigs.
